- **Cheapest MacBook ever** — $599 (₩990,000). The price of 109 Starbucks lattes gets you into the macOS ecosystem. "A MacBook under $600?"

| Missing | Why it matters | Pain level |

| **Backlit keyboard** | Can't type in dim cafes or dark rooms | 🔴🔴🔴 |

## Insight

**1. $599 MacBook = Apple's Market Share Play**

For the first time, Apple brought macOS entry pricing below $1,000. While Chromebooks dominated education, Apple insisted on $999+ MacBook Air. MacBook Neo is Apple saying "we have a $600 laptop too." It's not about margins — it's about ecosystem lock-in. Get them on macOS now, upsell later.

**2. A18 Pro Is a Recycled Chip — That's How $599 Is Possible**

The A18 Pro is already mass-produced for iPhone 16 Pro. No new M-series silicon design was needed — Apple reused an existing iPhone chip for a laptop. Near-zero incremental R&D cost. That's the real reason $599 works. Clever.

**3. It's Not "12 Missing Features" — It's What Remains That Matters**

MagSafe, Thunderbolt, backlit keyboard... the missing list looks long. But what's left is more important: **macOS + A18 Pro + 16-hour battery + 1.23 kg**. Those four things alone crush Chromebooks and budget Windows laptops. Apple defined the "minimum viable MacBook experience" — and it's surprisingly complete.

**4. Korea Premium 14–17% — Cheaper Products Get Higher Markups**

$599 × ₩1,450 = ₩868,550, but actual Korean price is ₩990,000 (+14%). MacBook Pro M5's premium is 9%. Apple charges proportionally more on budget products in Korea. "It's cheap, so a bigger markup still feels affordable" — that's the pricing psychology.

**5. MacBook Neo's Real Competitor Isn't MacBook Air — It's iPad**

$599 MacBook Neo vs $798 iPad Air M3 + Magic Keyboard. At similar price points, the choice is "keyboard-first macOS" vs "touchscreen iPadOS." Productivity → Neo. Content consumption + drawing → iPad. Apple competing with itself at this price tier is new and deliberate.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **A18 Pro** | Apple's chip designed for iPhone 16 Pro. 3nm (N3E) process, 6-core CPU (2 Performance + 4 Efficiency) + 5-core GPU. First A-series chip used in a Mac |
| **M4** | Apple's Mac-dedicated chip. 10-core CPU + 10-core GPU + 16GB+ RAM. Used in MacBook Air, MacBook Pro. Superior to A18 Pro in core count and memory |
| **sRGB vs P3** | Display color gamut standards. sRGB is the baseline; P3 (DCI-P3) covers 25% more colors. Critical for photo/video editors. Neo is estimated sRGB, Air is P3 |
| **MagSafe** | Apple's magnetic charging connector. Cable detaches safely if tripped over. Neo lacks it — charges via USB-C only |
| **Thunderbolt** | High-speed connection standard by Intel/Apple. Up to 40 Gb/s for external SSDs, displays. Same USB-C shape but 4x the speed |
| **Force Touch** | Apple's pressure-sensitive trackpad. Detects press depth for additional gestures. Neo uses a simpler Multi-Touch trackpad |
| **True Tone** | Auto-adjusts display color temperature to match ambient lighting. Reduces eye strain. Not available on MacBook Neo |
| **Liquid Retina** | Apple's brand name for high-resolution IPS LCD displays. Not OLED, but offers high resolution and color accuracy |
| **Fanless** | Passive cooling using the aluminum body as heatsink. Completely silent, but may throttle under sustained heavy load |
| **Apple Intelligence** | Apple's on-device AI features. Writing Tools, image generation, enhanced Siri. Runs on 8GB but advanced features may be limited |
